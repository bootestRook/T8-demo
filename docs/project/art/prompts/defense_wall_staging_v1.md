# defense_wall_staging_v1 Prompt

Use case: stylized-concept

Asset type: Godot 4 mobile portrait defense base sprite, source image for chroma-key cutout.

Primary request: Generate one integrated formal game art asset that combines the defense wall and the staging / ready area into one cohesive sprite. It must match the current in-game lower battlefield measurements: runtime sprite is 1080 px wide by 460 px tall. In local sprite coordinates, the visible top protrusions occupy roughly y 0-100, the actual gameplay wall band is y 100-220, and the staging/ready area is y 220-460. The actual gameplay wall rectangle remains only 1080x120; visual protrusions are allowed but must not imply a larger hit area.

Style reference: Match the provided screenshot style: vertical mobile game, sci-fi apocalypse city street defense line, dark grey hand-painted metal, concrete, mesh fencing, cyan machinery accents, small warm amber hazard lights, clean readable mobile-game silhouette, similar to the wall and lower base shown in the screenshot.

Scene/backdrop: perfectly flat solid #ff00ff chroma-key background for background removal. The background must be one uniform color with no shadows, no gradients, no texture, no floor plane, no lighting variation.

Subject: one continuous integrated base: a fortified city wall at the top connected seamlessly to a lower staging platform / operator deck. The top wall includes mesh fence panels, armored pillars, rails, bolts, pipes, chipped concrete, cyan-lit energy parts, and amber hazard plates. The lower staging area should be physically connected to the wall with matching materials: industrial floor panels, side mechanical columns, inset machinery, bolted plates, access platform, subtle grime, diagonal hazard markings, and a clear central deployment pad where the player character and weapon can be drawn by the game on top. Do not include any character, weapon, health bar, labels, UI, text, numbers, or icons.

Composition/framing: full-width horizontal asset centered on the canvas, viewed from top-down three-quarter perspective like a mobile tower defense battlefield. Wall and staging area must feel like one single constructed object with continuous seams, shadows, perspective, and material language. Leave transparent/padded space around irregular edges after chroma removal; keep the asset isolated.

Lighting/mood: consistent soft top-left game lighting, crisp edges, battle-worn but polished final production quality.

Color palette: charcoal and gunmetal, slate concrete, muted cyan/teal glow accents, small amber hazard lights, off-white worn floor panels, no bright green.

Constraints: pure flat #ff00ff background only; keep #ff00ff out of the asset; no cast shadow on the background; no contact shadow or reflection on the background; no text, no logos, no watermarks, no UI, no characters. The wall and staging area must be generated together as one unified sprite, not two separated pieces.
